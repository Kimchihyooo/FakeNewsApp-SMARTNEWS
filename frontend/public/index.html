How CredibilityScan Works?

How CredibilityScan acts as your digital detective to spot fake news.

01. The Readers

Text Analysis

The Problem: Sometimes people write stories that aren't true.

The Solution: We have two digital assistants named XLM - RoBERTa. They read every single word in the blink of an eye to understand the "feeling" of the storyâ€”whether it sounds like real news or a made-up lie.

02. The Accuracy Booster

XGBoost Classifier

The Problem: Sometimes one look isn't enough to catch a clever lie.

The Solution: We use XGBoost. Imagine a team of elite detectives working in a relay race. The first detective looks at the story and makes a guess. The second detective then looks only at the mistakes the first one made to fix them. They keep doing this until they have a nearly perfect answer!

03. The Face Checker

Deepfake Detection

The Problem: Videos can be edited to make people say things they never said.

The Solution: We use YOLOv8 (The Spotter) to find faces and MobileNetv2 (The Inspector) to look at tiny pixels. If the skin looks blurry or the lighting is weird, we know it's a trick.

04. The Analyst

Bias Distribution Analysis

The Problem: Not all real news is neutral. Accurate facts can still be manipulated through emotional language, political framing, or hidden agendas that sway public opinion.

The Solution: We measure the "stance" of the text. Our system calculates where the article falls on the political spectrum. It generates a simple graph showing if the content is **Leaning Left, Center (Neutral), or Leaning Right**, helping you instantly see the author's perspective.

05. The Highlighter

LIME (Local Interpretable Model-Agnostic Explanations)

The Problem: You need to know why something is fake.

The Solution: We use LIME. It's like a magic highlighter. We highlight suspicious sentences in Red, so you can see the proof yourself.

06. The Librarian

Cross-Referencing

The Problem: Is anyone else reporting this?

The Solution: We check the "Library" using Google Search. We look for your story on trusted news websites. If trusted sources are telling the same story, itâ€™s likely true.
